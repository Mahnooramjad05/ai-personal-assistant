The human body is composed of trillions of cells organized into tissues, organs, and organ systems. The circulatory system, driven by the heart, transports oxygen and nutrients through roughly 60,000 miles of blood vessels. The nervous system, centered on the brain and spinal cord, coordinates voluntary and involuntary actions using electrical and chemical signals carried by around 86 billion neurons. The skeletal system provides structural support with 206 bones in the adult human body, while the muscular system, made up of over 600 muscles, enables movement. The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients the body can absorb, and the immune system defends against pathogens using white blood cells and antibodies.
