Economics is the social science that studies how individuals, businesses, governments, and societies allocate scarce resources. It is broadly divided into microeconomics, which examines the behavior of individual households and firms, and macroeconomics, which studies aggregate phenomena such as inflation, unemployment, and economic growth. Key concepts include supply and demand, which determine market prices; opportunity cost, the value of the next best alternative given up when making a choice; and gross domestic product (GDP), a measure of the total economic output of a country. Inflation refers to a general rise in prices over time, reducing the purchasing power of money, while a recession is typically defined as a significant decline in economic activity lasting more than a few months.
