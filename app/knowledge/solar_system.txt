The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, including eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. It formed roughly 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud. The Sun contains about 99.86 percent of the Solar System's total mass. The inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) are rocky and relatively small, while the outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune) are much larger gas or ice giants. Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union.
